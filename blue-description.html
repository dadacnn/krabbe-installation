"Relative" #1 (Blue)

When I was around four years old, I lived in a housing complex in the suburbs of Tokyo. We moved away just as I was starting elementary school, so my memories of that time are hazy — but whenever I try to recall that period, the rooms always come back to me in shades of blue. The walls were white, the floors were wooden, there was nothing blue in that apartment at all. And yet.

My father was a domineering and manipulative man who would often scream at my mother. At his worst, he would throw things — a hair dryer, an ashtray. On nights when the fighting got that bad, I would lie in the next room wrapped in my duvet, praying that my mother would be okay.

One evening, as my parents were deep in one of their fights, I became convinced that I was the only one who could stop it. Steeling myself, I walked into the living room. I was terrified, barely holding back tears, and I told them to please stop fighting. My father turned to me and said, "Children should stay out of it" — then turned straight back to my mother and kept going. I was overwhelmed, a mess of grief at not being able to help her and rage at him, and I slid down beneath the piano stool in the living room and cried and cried.

I don't remember clearly how it ended. But it remains one of the earliest and most vivid memories of my life.
