"Relative" #2 (Red)

Exactly one week after I turned four, my brother was born.

One afternoon, not long after, I was making my way to the living room when I noticed something at the center of the room — a small bundle wrapped in a red blanket, sitting there on its own. That's my brother, I thought. I still remember how vividly that blazing red stood out against everything around it. I crept closer and held still to listen. I could hear him breathing in his sleep. Without even touching him, I could sense the warmth and dampness radiating from his skin — so alive that I half-expected to see steam rising from him if I looked hard enough.

The light coming through the glass window fell over that small space and made it feel warm and still, yet somehow sacred. Even as a child, I think I felt in my body the mystery of what a baby is.

In that moment, I think I encountered a life outside of myself for the first time. Until then, I had always been the center of my own world, doing as I pleased — but something shifted, and I became aware that the world did not belong to me alone. There was a loneliness in it, a sense of one chapter of childhood drawing quietly to a close. And yet it was also a profound joy, because someone had come to join me.
